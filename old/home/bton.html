So since I've been in Bloomington for the past 12 or so years, I guess I must like it here. Coming down fresh out of high school from Indianapolis, Bloomington seemed like paradise. Home to Indiana University, Bloomington offers the cultural enrichment and nightlife of a University combined with the closeness of a small town. I've gone without a car for years on end without feeling any great inconvenience. Most residents say what they like about Bloomington is the fact that there is enough going on here to have a reasonable nightlife, but still small enough that you know people almost every time you walk downtown. Another attraction is the proximity of Yellowwood National Forest, only about 20 minutes from downtown.

Food and Fun

 If you ask me, the best place to go to eat is the Uptown Cafe, but I would also recommend Lennie's, the Laughing Planet, The Trojan Horse, Brave New Deli, Bear's Place, and investigating the wide array of ethnic restaurants in the Kirkwood area. For coffee, I usually go to Soma and sometimes the Runciple Spoon. To see music(check out my Local Music Page). I usually go to 2nd Story unless there's a good band at the Bluebird. Other places for music include Bear's Place(which features Jazz Fables, a weekly Jazz show put on every Thursday), Rhino's all-ages, Vertigo, Space 101, The John Waldron Arts Center, The Buskirk-Chumley(Buskirk) Theatre, Uncle Fester's, and the City Grill. The Waldron and the Buskirk are also good places to see plays, which you can also find at the Bloomington Playwrites' Project, on campus at I.U., and in 3rd Street Park in the summer. Bear's Place, together with I.U., also hosts the Ryder Film Series, which specializes in the coolest second-run/foreign/classic films out there. If you're looking to curl up with a good book, Howard's Bookstore, on the South side of the square, has a good selection of books, as well as a cat or two wandering around; The Book Corner, at the corner of Kirkwood and Walnut, has some fine books; and Boxcar Books has something for the more politically minded. Alternative videos and DVDs can be found at Plan 9, on 6th between Walnut and Washington, and a new video store, Cinemat offers "Fine Video & DVD Rentals" as well as movie screenings.

Indiana University

 I don't want to discount the cultural offerings of I.U., though I think supporting local stuff to be important. The Musical Arts Center(MAC) is the only place I know of to see a symphony or an opera in town. They also bring Broadway plays and world-famous entertainers to the I.U. Auditorium . Smaller up-and-coming bands are often booked to play in the I.U. Memorial Union. Art from throughout the world and history can be found in the I.U. Art Museum and student work and travelling shows can be found in the School of Fine Arts(SOFA) Gallery. Student recitals can be heard on Sundays at Auer Hall.

Radio

 I don't listen to the radio much, but I think it's really cool that we have a community radio station in Bloomington, WFHB, at 91.3 and 98.1 FM. They play a lot of cool stuff that you don't hear on commercial radio. Broadcasting from I.U., we have WFIU at 103.7 FM, offering NPR content and WIUS at 1570 AM, offering Pure Student Radio. The commercial options are not bad either, with WTTS at 92.3 playing rock and B97(WBWB) at 96.7 FM playing dance and hits.

Periodicals

 To keep up with the happenings around town, check out monthly Ryder Magazine, Culture Week, or the politically-driven Bloomington Alternative. Newspapers include the Bloomington Herald Times and the Indiana Daily Student.

Galleries

 Places to find original art in Bloomington include The Belleview Gallery at 312 S. Washington, The By-Hand Gallery in Fountain Square Mall, and The Gallery at 109 E. 6th St. I.U. also has it's art museum and School of Fine Arts Gallery as well as the Lilly Library. Original art can also be found lining the walls of such establishments as Michael's Uptown Cafe, The Laughing Planet, The Runciple Spoon, and many other places.
